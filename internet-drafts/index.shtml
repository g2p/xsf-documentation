XMPP Internet-Drafts

Last Updated: 2008-05-07

The base XMPP specifications are RFC 3920 and RFC 3921. While most XMPP extensions are defined in the XEP series published by the XMPP Standards Foundation, the following XMPP-related Internet-Drafts are under consideration within the IETF:

  • draft-ietf-sieve-notify-xmpp-09 (text | HTML | feed) -- XMPP notifications for the Sieve email filtering language (approved for publication on 2008-02-23)

  • draft-saintandre-atompub-notify-07 (text | HTML) -- transporting Atom syndication data over the XMPP pubsub extension

  • draft-saintandre-header-im-01 (text | HTML) -- an email header field for IM URIs

  • draft-saintandre-header-pres-01 (text | HTML) -- an email header field for presence URIs

  • draft-saintandre-jabberid-08 (text | HTML) -- an email header field for Jabber IDs

  • draft-saintandre-rfc3920bis-04 (text | HTML) -- proposed revisions to RFC 3920

  • draft-saintandre-rfc3921bis-04 (text | HTML) -- proposed revisions to RFC 3921

  • draft-saintandre-sip-xmpp-chat-00 (text | HTML) -- mappings for interoperability between XMPP and the Message Session Relay Protocol

  • draft-saintandre-sip-xmpp-core-00 (text | HTML) -- architectural assumptions and address mappings underlying interworking between between XMPP and SIP

  • draft-saintandre-sip-xmpp-im-00 (text | HTML) -- mappings for interworking between between XMPP and SIMPLE IM

  • draft-saintandre-sip-xmpp-media-00 (text | HTML) -- mappings for interworking between between the XMPP Jingle extensions and SIP

  • draft-saintandre-sip-xmpp-presence-00 (text | HTML) -- mappings for interworking between between XMPP and SIMPLE presence

  • draft-saintandre-xmpp-feature-set-00 (text | HTML) -- a protocol feature set for interoperability testing of XMPP technologies

  • draft-saintandre-xmpp-presence-analysis-03 (text | HTML) -- an analysis of the scalability of interdomain presence federation using XMPP, for comparision with draft-ietf-simple-interdomain-scaling-analysis

Older versions of these and other drafts are available in the attic (see also the source control repository).
