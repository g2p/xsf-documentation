About XMPP

Summary: The Extensible Messaging and Presence Protocol (XMPP) is an open XML technology for real-time communication, which powers a wide range of applications including instant messaging, presence, media negotiation, whiteboarding, collaboration, lightweight middleware, content syndication, and generalized XML routing.

History: The core technology was invented by Jeremie Miller in 1998, refined in the Jabber open-source community in 1999 and 2000, and formalized by the IETF in 2002 and 2003, resulting in publication of RFC 3920 and RFC 3921 in 2004.

Discussion: Although the core technology is stable, the XMPP community continues to define various XMPP extensions through an open process run by the XMPP Standards Foundation, and there is an active community of open-source and commercial developers based at jabber.org.
