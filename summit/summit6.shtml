XMPP Summit 6

The sixth XMPP Summit to be held by the XMPP Standards Foundation will occur on February 7-9, 2009, in Brussels, Belgium (the same weekend as FOSDEM 2009).

Some logistical details are still being worked out (join the summit@xmpp.org list for discussion), but the tentative schedule is as follows:

Friday, February 6: Jingle Hackfest

A Jingle hackfest for developers who wish to code and test their implemenations of Jingle (primarily voice and video, but we will also discuss file transfer and other use cases). The tentative schedule is 10:00 to 4:00 (location to be determined -- a hotel downtown, not at the FOSDEM location).

If you would like to participate in the Jingle hackfest, please say so on the jingle@xmpp.org mailing list or contact Peter Saint-Andre.

Saturday, February 7: Devroom @ FOSDEM

From noon until 6 PM we will have a devroom where we can host tutorials, presentations, demos, and lightning talks at FOSDEM 2009. If you are interested in being a speaker, please contact Peter Saint-Andre. We are especially interested in technical talks of interest to open-source geeks. No marketing fluff, please! The typical talk will be 15 minutes with 15 minutes for discussion.

The following people have volunteered to make presentations:

  • Peter Saint-Andre (XSF) and Remko Tronçon (Psi): XMPP 101
  • Nathan Fritz, Seesmic: PubSub and the Web
  • Jack Moffitt (StanzIQ): Integrating XMPP into Web Technologies
  • Dirk Meyer (TZI): Personal Media Networks
  • Simon Tennant (BuddyCloud): Geolocation
  • Diana Cionoiu (Yate): Jingle / VoIP
  • Florian Jensen (Flosoft): Large Scale XMPP
  • Dave Cridland (Isode): Presenting Information Flow in Deployed XMPP Clients

Sunday, February 8: FOSDEM

Developers are free to attend the second day of the FOSDEM conference. We'll need help manning the booth and attending talks to spread the word about XMPP. Also expect many interesting "hallway" discussions and continued hacking in the background.

Monday, February 9: XMPP Summit

The official "XMPP Summit" itself: Intensive discussions among core XMPP developers to solve pressing problems in the XMPP protocol stack. The main topics will probably be mobile optimizations, file transfer, and end-to-end encryption as recently prioritized by the XMPP Council.

We have limited space for the Summit and it is not intended for people who are just interested in learning about XMPP (see Saturday schedule), but instead for active technical contributors in the XMPP community. If you would like to participate, please say so on the summit@xmpp.org mailing list or contact Peter Saint-Andre. The tentative schedule is 9:00 to 3:00 (location to be determined -- a hotel downtown, not at the FOSDEM location).
