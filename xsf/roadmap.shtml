XSF Roadmap

Author:Peter Saint-Andre
Version:0.11
Date:2009-03-19

The XSF has identified several high-priority initiatives to help achieve that mission:

  1. Complete definition of Jingle as an open technology for generalized session negotiation (XEP-0166), including application types for voice and video via RTP (XEP-0167) and file transfer (XEP-0234) as well as transport methods for ICE (XEP-0176), raw UDP associations (XEP-0177), SOCKS5 bytestreams (XEP-0260), and in-band bytestreams (XEP-0261).

  2. Incorporate implementation and deployment experience into the core definition of XMPP by completing revisions to rfc3920bis and rfc3921bis.

  3. Define a method for secure, implementable, user-friendly, end-to-end encryption over XMPP (one proposed approach is summarized in draft-meyer-xmpp-e2e-encryption).

  4. Define several optimizations to XMPP's core streaming, presence, and roster protocols for improved performance in mobile environments (mainly XEP-0198: Stream Management and XEP-0237: Roster Versioning).

  5. Complete definition of protocols and best practices for prevention of spam, phishing, abuse, and denial of service attacks on the XMPP network (mainly XEP-0159: Spim-Blocking Control, XEP-0161: Abuse Reporting, and XEP-0165: Best Practices to Discourage JID Mimicking).
