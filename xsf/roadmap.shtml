XSF Roadmap

Author:Peter Saint-Andre
Version:0.10
Date:2009-03-11

The XSF has identified several high-priority initiatives to help achieve that mission:

  1. Complete definition of Jingle as an open technology for voice, video, and generalized session negotiation.

  2. Incorporate implementation and deployment experience into the core definition of XMPP by completing revisions to rfc3920bis and rfc3921bis.

  3. Define a method for secure, implementable, user-friendly end-to-end encryption over XMPP (the proposed approach is summarized in draft-meyer-xmpp-e2e-encryption).

  4. Define several optimizations to XMPP's core streaming, presence, and roster protocols for improved performance in mobile environments (mainly XEP-0198: Stream Management and XEP-0237: Roster Versioning).

  5. Complete definition of protocols and best practices for prevention of spam, phishing, denial of service attacks, and abuse on the XMPP network (see XEP-0159: Spim-Blocking Control, XEP-0161: Abuse Reporting, and XEP-0165: Best Practices to Discourage JID Mimicking).

  6. Following up on the establishment of an intermediate certification authority for the XMPP network, deploy ubiquitous channel encryption on the open network of XMPP servers.

  7. Continue to develop a complete suite of XMPP extensions for real-time communication and collaboration, and encourage integration of those extensions into widely deployed open-source technologies and Internet services. Current areas of focus are:

    • Achieve of consensus regarding go-forward file transfer technologies (XEP-0234: Jingle File Transfer).
    • Encourage widespread deployment of the personal eventing profile of publish-subscribe.
