XSF Roadmap

Author:Peter Saint-Andre
Version:0.2
Date:2007-01-08

The overall goal of the XMPP Standards Foundation (XSF) is to build an open, standardized, secure, feature-rich, widely-deployed, decentralized infrastructure for real-time communication in order to successfully supplant or co-opt proprietary, closed, centralized services such as AIM, MSN, Yahoo!, and Skype. The XSF has identified several high-priority initiatives for 2006-2007 to help achieve that goal:

  1. Finish definition of Jingle as an open technology for federated voice, video, and generalized multimedia communication, and encourage implementation in high-profile open-source client codebases (Q1 2007).

  2. Hold second and third XMPP interoperability testing events (Q1 and Q3 2007) and launch a secure online environment for ongoing XMPP testing over the Internet (Q1 2007).

  3. Complete revisions to RFC 3920 and RFC 3921 and advance them to a status of Draft Standard at the IETF (Q2 or Q3 2007).

  4. Complete development of robust technologies for prevention of spam on the XMPP network (Q3 or Q4 2007).

  5. Finish definition of encrypted sessions as the go-forward technology for end-to-end encryption of XMPP communications, and encourage implementation in high-profile open-source client and library codebases (Q3 or Q4 2007).

  6. Deploy ubiquitous channel encryption on the open network of XMPP servers (Q4 2007).

  7. Establish the XSF as an intermediate CA for the XMPP server network through one or more root CAs (Q4 2006). (complete)

  8. Clarify the branding of Jabber and XMPP, including migration of the XEP series to www.xmpp.org and re-launching of www.jabber.org (Q1 2007). (complete)
