XSF Privacy and Security Policy

Author:Peter Saint-Andre
Version:0.3
Date:2008-10-15
Status:Working Draft

The XMPP Standards Foundation (XSF) is committed to the privacy and security of your information and communications. This Privacy and Security Policy governs all of the Internet services provided by the XSF, including the xmpp.org and jabber.org websites as well as the instant messaging service hosted at jabber.org.

What Information We Gather

We do not gather any personal information (name, address, etc.) about you at jabber.org and xmpp.org; the only exceptions are information that you may voluntarily submit, such as your vCard profile.

When you create an instant messaging account at the jabber.org IM service, you provide a username and password. We do not gather any other personal information about you when you create an account.

When you add people to your contact list (XMPP "roster"), that information is stored on the server so you can retrieve it whenever you log in; the roster information can contain the names or nicknames of your contacts if you assign such names in your roster.

The jabber.org instant messaging service supports an XMPP extension for storing data on the server: the Private XML Storage protocol. If your Jabber client also supports this feature, it might store bookmarked chatrooms, client preferences, and other data on the server. However, this data is never made public and is accessible only by you. Please check with the developers of your preferred Jabber client software about their support for this feature.

The jabber.org instant messaging service also supports an XMPP extension for publishing data through the server: the Personal Eventing Protocol. If your Jabber client also supports this feature and you configure your client to enable that feature, it might use the jabber.org service as a way to publish information about the tunes you listen to on your computer, your mood, and other "rich presence" data; furthermore, this data may be temporarily stored in our database so that it can be retrieved by your contacts when they log in. This data is never made public unless you so choose, and by default is accessible only by your contacts. Please check with the developers of your preferred Jabber client software about their support for this feature.

The jabber.org instant messaging service does not log information about your Internet Protocol (IP) address when you connect to the service. However, we reserve the right to block specific IP addresses that pose a threat or cause harm to our network services, and to keep a list of such offending IP addresses; this list of blacklisted IPs will not be made public.

Passwords

Passwords are not required to access any of the jabber.org or xmpp.org websites.

Passwords are required to access the jabber.org IM service. We strong encourage you to log in with an encrypted connection so that your password is not exposed to eavesdroppers; you can easily do this by connecting on the legacy port 5223 for Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) or by upgrading your connection on the standard port 5222 to an encrypted connection using the Transport Layer Security (TLS) protocol. The XSF may in the near future mandate this support and disallow unencrypted connections.

Please note that currently your account password on the jabber.org IM service is stored as plaintext, not in hashed or encrypted form. Although access to the machine on which the jabber.org IM service runs is highly restricted and protected, any of the trusted administrators with access to the machine can view your password, and if the machine is hacked then the hacker would be able to learn your password. For this reason, we strongly encourage you to use a password here that you do not use elsewhere. We are also exploring options for encrypting your IM password, or for doing away with passwords altogether.

Private Messages

Private messages are IMs that you send to your friends on the jabber.org service or on other XMPP-based services on the Internet (such as Google Talk). If your messages are sent through other services then it is possible that those services can log your messages, and we do not have control over those services. However, your private messages are never intentionally logged at the jabber.org service.

Chatrooms

The XSF hosts a number of XMPP chatrooms at the conference.jabber.org domain. Conversations in some of these rooms are archived for future reference at <http://logs.jabber.org/> -- please visit that link to find out which rooms are archived. If you do not want your chatroom messages archived, please do not join these specific rooms. All other rooms on conference.jabber.org are unlogged, and logging can be enabled only by asking the server admins to enable logging (please ask in the jabber@conference.jabber.org room).

Discussion Lists

The XSF hosts a number of email discussion lists at jabber.org and xmpp.org using the common Mailman list manager software. All messages to these lists are archived for future reference. If you do not want your messages archived, please do not post to these lists.

Cookies

Some of our websites are deployed using common software packages such as MediaWiki and WordPress, which use cookies for session IDs and other features. If you are not comfortable with these cookies, we encourage you to disable or track them using a common web browser plugin such as CookieSafe for Mozilla Firefox.

Scripts

Some of our websites use JavaScript to provide enhanced functionality, such as the ability to dynamically change the order of information presented in tables. If you are not comfortable with these scripts, we encourage you to disable them using a common web browser plugin such as NoScript for Mozilla Firefox.

Changes to This Policy

Any potential changes to this policy will be posted at least one month in advance to the public juser@jabber.org discussion list (also accessible via web forum and news group).

How to Contact Us

If you have any questions or suggestions regarding our Privacy and Security Policy, please send email to privacy@xmpp.org or to the public juser@jabber.org discussion list.
